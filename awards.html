Awards

ACNS offers two types of awards:

1. Student Travel Awards

Huge congratulations to our 10 Student Travel Award winners. Each will receive $250 to support their travel to the Shoal Bay conference, generously donated by the ARC Centre for Excellence in Cognition and Disorders (CCD). It was a very competitive year, with many fantastic applications submitted. Thank you to all those who applied. Our winners are:

  • Alie Male, Murdock University
  • Aimee Martin, University of Queensland
  • Amy Maddock, Victoria University of Wellington
  • Daniel Feuerriegel, University of South Australia
  • Elizabeth Thomas, Monash University
  • Jonathan Robinson, Queensland University of Technology
  • Julian Matthews, Monash University
  • Noam Gordon, Monash University
  • Manuela Russo, Queensland University of Technology
  • Tijl Grootswagers, Macquarie University

2. Presentation awards

Junior post-doctoral researchers and students are also eligible for oral and poster presentation awards.

To be eligible for a presentation award, post-docs and students must be:

  • A current student (PhD, Masters, Honours, undergraduate) or have graduated with a PhD in the last 12 months
  • The presenting (first) author
  • A current ACNS student member
  • Registered for ACNS2016

Presentation awards will be presented at the closing of the conference before the AGM.
